This page exists to describe the kind of community Karyon is actually trying to build at this stage. The project does not have a sprawling public network, a large mentorship program, or a mature research collective. What it does have is a founding-stage opportunity to gather people who care about biological intelligence, learning, systems design, and rigorous public reasoning enough to help shape the project before its culture hardens.

## Founding-stage community posture

Karyon should not optimize for a generic AI audience or for the broadest possible reach. The better goal is a smaller, more aligned group of people who are willing to think carefully, challenge assumptions honestly, and tolerate the uncertainty that comes with a theory-rich project still becoming real.

That means the community posture should favor seriousness over hype, public reasoning over vague enthusiasm, and alignment over scale. If the project attracts fewer people but the right kinds of people, that is a healthier outcome than building a large audience around an inflated story.

## Who this project is for

Karyon is likely to resonate with people who are interested in some combination of:

- cognitive architecture beyond standard agent harness patterns
- distributed systems as a substrate for intelligence
- psychology, biology, and learning theory as sources of architectural insight
- graph memory, temporal state, and interpretability
- open-ended but disciplined research conversations
- building in public without pretending certainty where it does not exist

It is also a good fit for contributors who enjoy tightening language, pressure-testing architecture, and helping a project become more legible rather than simply adding more surface area.

## Who may find it frustrating

This project may be frustrating if you want a polished product roadmap, a near-term SaaS narrative, or a simple claim that bigger models plus better tools are enough. It may also be frustrating if you want a community optimized for speed over reflection, consensus over critique, or growth over intellectual coherence.

Karyon is still working out deep questions about what kind of intelligence stack it even wants to become. That makes it exciting for some people and too open-ended for others.

## What kinds of collaboration are wanted now

The most useful community energy right now is concrete and disciplined.

- pressure-test the architectural thesis
- challenge unclear or inflated language
- compare the public story against the code
- contribute implementation realism where theory runs ahead
- synthesize related research that sharpens or falsifies key assumptions
- help develop norms for careful public discussion around difficult ideas

The project benefits most from people who can bring relevant but diverse perspectives without trying to collapse everything back into the most familiar AI patterns.

## How public discussion should work

At this stage, the healthiest public discussion surfaces are the ones closest to the work itself. Repository discussions, issues, notes, and source-linked writing are more valuable than scattering attention across channels that imply activity without sustaining it.

That is why the current public surface stays simple. It is better to have a small number of real places for thoughtful exchange than to imply a mature network of forums, events, and programs that are not yet active.

## How future community surfaces should be added

More community infrastructure should be added only when it can be maintained honestly. Live chat, working groups, reading circles, or updates infrastructure may all make sense later, but they should appear because the project has real energy to support them, not because those are the default trappings of a modern project site.

## What feedback is most useful here

The best feedback on this page is whether it filters for the right people. Does it invite serious collaborators without sounding closed or self-important? Does it make the project’s stage and standards clear? If you want the practical ways to help right now, continue to [contribute](/contribute/). If you want a more candid running log of the project as it evolves, read the [notes](/notes/).
